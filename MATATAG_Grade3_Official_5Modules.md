## üìö THE 5 OFFICIAL MODULES

### **MODULE 1: Phonics and Word Study**
**Subtitle**: *Sounds to Words*
**Code**: EN3PWS

#### Learning Competencies

##### A. Sight Words Recognition
- **EN3PWS-I/II/III/IV-1**: Identify Grade 3 level-appropriate sight words
- Read and recognize sight words automatically

##### B. Word Patterns (Quarter 1 & 2)
Master reading words according to patterns:

**Quarter 1:**
1. **CVCC words** (Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Consonant)
   - Examples: jump, tent, milk, fast, hand

2. **CCVC words** (Consonant-Consonant-Vowel-Consonant) - clusters and diphthongs
   - Examples: stop, grab, flip, trip, clap

3. **VCV words** (Vowel-Consonant-Vowel)
   - Examples: open, baby, music, tiger

4. **VCCV words** (Vowel-Consonant-Consonant-Vowel)
   - Examples: apple, kitten, butter, sister

**Quarter 2:**
- Continue VCV and VCCV patterns
- Advanced word pattern recognition

**Quarters 3-4:**
- Mastery and fluency with all word patterns

#### Key Skills
- Read words accurately and automatically
- Decode unfamiliar words using phonics patterns
- Recognize sight words instantly (no sounding out)

### **MODULE 2: Vocabulary and Word Knowledge**
**Subtitle**: *Words*
**Code**: EN3VWK

#### Learning Competencies

##### A. High-Frequency Words
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-1**: Identify high-frequency words accurately
- Build vocabulary of 300-500+ common words

##### B. Thematic Vocabulary
- **EN3VWK-I/II-2**: Use vocabulary referring to:
  1. **Regional themes** (Q1-Q2): Local culture, places, traditions
  2. **National themes** (Q3-Q4): Philippine heroes, national symbols, culture
  3. **Content-specific topics**: Academic subjects

##### C. Content-Specific Words
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-3**: Use content-specific words from:
  1. **Mathematics**: Basic symbols and terminologies
     - Examples: add, subtract, multiply, divide, sum, difference, equal
  2. **Science**: Basic terminologies
     - Examples: observe, experiment, classify, living things, non-living

##### D. Word Functions
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-4**: Identify words with different functions:

**1. Action Words (Verbs)**
- Words that label actions (doing words)
- Examples: run, jump, eat, play, write, read

**2. Describing Words (Adjectives)**
- Words that describe persons, places, things, animals, events, ideas, emotions
- Examples: big, small, happy, sad, red, beautiful

**3. Pronouns** (Q1-Q2)
- Words that replace nouns:
  - **a. Interrogative pronouns**: who, what, which, whose
  - **b. Possessive pronouns**: my, mine, your, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
  - **c. Demonstrative pronouns**: this, that, these, those

##### E. Word Relationships
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-5**: Identify synonyms and antonyms
  - **Synonyms**: big/large, happy/joyful, start/begin
  - **Antonyms**: hot/cold, tall/short, day/night

##### F. Word Roots
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-8**: Identify roots of high-frequency words
  - Focus on nouns, verbs, and adjectives
  - Example: play ‚Üí plays, played, playing, player

##### G. Reading and Writing Words
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-6**: Read words correctly for meaning (based on word patterns)
- **EN3VWK-I/II/III/IV-7**: Write words legibly and correctly (based on word patterns)

#### Key Skills
- Recognize 300-500+ high-frequency words
- Use context clues to understand new words
- Identify parts of speech
- Understand word relationships

### **MODULE 3: Grammar Awareness and Grammatical Structures**
**Subtitle**: *Sentences*
**Code**: EN3GAGS

#### Learning Competencies

##### A. Sentence Recognition
- **EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-1**: Identify sentences and non-sentences
  - Sentence: Complete thought with subject and verb
  - Non-sentence: Incomplete thought or fragment

##### B. Simple Sentences

**1. Using Simple Sentences**
- **EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-2**: Use simple sentences to express ideas about regional/national themes
- **EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-3**: Use simple sentences with proper intonation (pitch, juncture, rhythm)

**2. Sentence Types (EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-4)**
Sequence words to represent meaning in:
1. **Telling sentences (Declarative)**: The dog is big.
2. **Asking sentences (Interrogative)**: Where is the dog?
3. **Commanding/Requesting sentences (Imperative)**: Close the door.
4. **Exclamatory sentences**: What a big dog!

**3. Parts of Simple Sentences (EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-5)**
Identify parts: who/what, what are they doing, when/where/how
- For all 4 sentence types
- Subject, predicate, and additional information

**4. Capitalization and Punctuation (EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-6)**
Use correct capitalization and punctuation for:
1. **Declarative**: Capital at start, period at end (.)
2. **Interrogative**: Capital at start, question mark at end (?)
3. **Imperative**: Capital at start, period at end (.)
4. **Exclamatory**: Capital at start, exclamation point at end (!)

##### C. Compound Sentences (Starting Quarter 2)

**1. Understanding Compound Sentences (EN3GAGS-II/III/IV-7)**
Identify in a compound sentence:
1. The two independent clauses
2. The joining word (coordinating conjunction): and, but, or, so
3. The doers and the actions in the two clauses

Example: "I like apples, **but** I love oranges."
- Clause 1: I like apples
- Clause 2: I love oranges
- Conjunction: but

**2. Punctuation in Compound Sentences (EN3GAGS-II/III/IV-8)**
- Use comma before coordinating conjunction
- Proper capitalization

##### D. Discourse Markers (EN3GAGS-I/II/III/IV-7 & 9)
Identify discourse markers for text types:
1. **Time order and procedural**: first, next, then, finally, after
2. **Description**: for example, such as, looks like
3. **Explanation**: because, so, as a result, therefore

#### Key Skills
- Construct grammatically correct simple sentences
- Form compound sentences with proper conjunctions
- Use correct punctuation and capitalization
- Identify parts of speech in context

### **MODULE 4: Comprehending and Analyzing Text**
**Subtitle**: *Discourse - Understanding Texts*
**Code**: EN3CAT

#### Learning Competencies

##### A. Reading Fluency
- **EN3CAT-I/II/III/IV-1**: Read grade level sentences with:
  - **Appropriate speed**: 80-100 words per minute (Grade 3 target)
  - **Accuracy**: 95%+ correct word recognition
  - **Expression**: Proper intonation and phrasing

##### B. Comprehending Stories (Narrative Texts)
**EN3CAT-I/II/III/IV-2**: Comprehend stories

**1. Note Story Elements**
- Characters: Who is in the story?
- Setting: Where and when does the story happen?
- Events: What happens in the story?

**2. Sequence Events**
- Q1-Q2: Sequence at least **four to five events**
- Q3-Q4: Sequence at least **five events**
- Use time order words (first, next, then, finally)

**3. Identify Problem and Solution**
- What is the main problem in the story?
- How is the problem solved?

**4. Infer Character's Feelings and Traits**
- How does the character feel? (happy, sad, angry, scared)
- What kind of person is the character? (brave, kind, selfish, helpful)
- Use evidence from text

**5. Relate Story Events to Experience**
- Connect story to personal experiences
- Make text-to-self connections

**6. Identify Cause and Effect**
- What happened? (Effect)
- Why did it happen? (Cause)

**7. Predict Possible Ending**
- What might happen next?
- How might the story end?
- Use clues from the story

**8. Give a Summary**
- Retell main events in order
- Include beginning, middle, and end
- Use own words

##### C. Comprehending Informational Texts
**EN3CAT-I/II/III/IV-3**: Comprehend informational texts

**1. Note Significant Details**
- Q1-Q2: At least **four to five significant details**
- Q3-Q4: At least **five significant details**
- Identify main idea and supporting details

**2. Identify Problem and Solution**
- What is the problem described?
- What are the solutions presented?

**3. Identify Text Types**
Recognize and understand:

a. **Time Order and Procedural**
   - How-to texts
   - Step-by-step instructions
   - Sequence of events
   - Signal words: first, second, next, then, finally

b. **Description**
   - Describes a person, place, thing, or idea
   - Uses sensory details
   - Signal words: looks like, for example, such as, including

c. **Explanation**
   - Explains why or how something happens
   - Cause and effect relationships
   - Signal words: because, so, as a result, therefore, due to

**4. Draw Conclusions**
- Make inferences based on information
- Use evidence from text
- Think critically about what was read

#### Key Skills
- Read fluently at grade level (80-100 wpm)
- Identify story elements (character, setting, plot)
- Sequence 4-5 events in order
- Make inferences and predictions
- Identify cause and effect
- Summarize texts in own words
- Distinguish between text types

#### Text Distribution (IMPORTANT)
Based on MATATAG requirements:
- **70% Narrative texts**: Stories, fables, myths, legends, narrative poems
- **30% Informational texts**: Articles, how-to, descriptions, explanations

### **MODULE 5: Creating and Composing Text**
**Subtitle**: *Discourse - Creating Texts*
**Code**: EN3CCT

#### Learning Competencies

##### A. Social Language Use
- **EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-1**: Use common expressions and polite greetings appropriate to a given situation
  - Good morning, Good afternoon, Please, Thank you
  - Excuse me, I'm sorry, You're welcome
  - May I...?, Can I...?, Could you...?

##### B. Retelling Literary Texts
- **EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-2**: Use own words in retelling:
  - Myths (stories that explain natural phenomena)
  - Legends (stories about heroes or historical events)
  - Fables (short stories with moral lessons)
  - Narrative poems

##### C. Expressing Personal Experiences
**EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-3**: Express ideas about one's experiences

**Themes by Quarter:**
1. **Regional themes** (Q1-Q2)
   - Local festivals and celebrations
   - Regional food and traditions
   - Local landmarks and places

2. **National themes** (Q3-Q4)
   - Philippine heroes
   - National symbols (flag, anthem)
   - Filipino culture and values

3. **Content-specific topics** (All quarters)
   - Math, Science, Social Studies topics

##### D. Using Academic Vocabulary
- **EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-4**: Use basic sight words, high frequency, and content-specific words
  - Apply vocabulary learned in Module 2
  - Use grade-appropriate words in writing

##### E. Composing Reactions and Responses
**Reacting to Stories (EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-5)**
Compose texts to react to:
- Character: "I like the character because..."
- Setting: "The story happens in..."
- Events: "I think the event was..."

**Reacting to Topics (EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-6)**
- Compose texts to react to a topic
- Express opinions and ideas
- Support with reasons

##### F. Summarizing
- **EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-7**: Make a summary of narrative text
  - Retell main events in sequence
  - Include beginning, middle, end
  - Use own words

##### G. Expressing Ideas Using Text Types
**EN3CCT-I/II/III/IV-8**: Express ideas using text types

**1. Time Order and Procedural**
- Write step-by-step instructions
- How-to texts
- Sequence events in order
- Use: first, next, then, finally

Example:
```
How to Make a Sandwich
First, get two slices of bread.
Next, spread peanut butter on one slice.
Then, spread jelly on the other slice.
Finally, put the slices together.
```

**2. Description**
- Describe a person, place, thing, or animal
- Use adjectives and sensory details
- Paint a picture with words

Example:
```
My Dog
My dog is big and brown. He has soft fur and a long tail.
His name is Max. He likes to play and run in the park.
```

**3. Explanation**
- Explain why something happens
- Explain how something works
- Use cause-effect relationships

Example:
```
Why Plants Need Water
Plants need water to live. Water helps them grow big and strong.
Without water, plants will become dry and die.
```

#### Key Skills
- Use polite expressions in context
- Retell stories in own words
- Write about personal experiences
- Compose reactions to texts
- Summarize narrative texts
- Write using different text types (procedural, descriptive, explanatory)

## üìä Quarterly Progression

### Quarter 1 (Regional Themes)
- **Focus**: Basic skills foundation
- Simple sentences
- CVCC, CCVC, VCV, VCCV word patterns
- Story comprehension (4-5 events)
- Regional vocabulary and themes

### Quarter 2 (Regional Themes)
- **Focus**: Building on basics
- Introduction to **compound sentences**
- Continue word patterns (VCV, VCCV)
- Deeper comprehension strategies
- More complex regional themes

### Quarter 3 (National Themes)
- **Focus**: National identity
- Mastery of simple and compound sentences
- Sight word fluency
- 5-event sequencing
- National heroes, symbols, culture

### Quarter 4 (National Themes)
- **Focus**: Mastery and integration
- All sentence types with confidence
- Full text type awareness
- Advanced comprehension
- National pride and values

## üìö Curriculum Codes Reference
