## üìö MODULE 1: Oral Language & Phonological Awareness

### MATATAG Alignment
- **Subdomain 1**: Oral Language
- **Subdomain 2**: Phonological Awareness

### Description
Develop speaking, listening, and sound recognition skills

### Learning Competencies (Grade 3)

#### A. Speaking Skills
- Express ideas, feelings, and opinions clearly
- Tell/retell stories with proper sequence
- Ask and answer questions appropriately
- Give clear directions and instructions
- Participate actively in class discussions
- Use appropriate volume and tone
- Speak in complete sentences

#### B. Listening Skills
- Follow multi-step oral directions (3-4 steps)
- Listen attentively during read-alouds
- Identify main ideas in oral presentations
- Recall details from what was heard
- Show understanding through responses

#### C. Phonological Awareness
- Identify and produce rhyming words
- Count syllables in multi-syllabic words (up to 4 syllables)
- Blend and segment phonemes in words
- Identify beginning, middle, and ending sounds
- Manipulate sounds (add, delete, substitute phonemes)
- Recognize word families (-at, -an, -it, etc.)

### Activity Types
- üé§ Speaking exercises and oral presentations
- üëÇ Listening comprehension activities
- üéµ Rhyming games and sound matching
- üîä Syllable counting exercises
- üó£Ô∏è Role-playing conversations
- üìª Audio story retelling

### Mastery Criteria
- 80% accuracy in sound identification
- Can follow 3-step oral directions
- Successfully retells simple stories
- Participates in conversations appropriately

## üìñ MODULE 2: Phonics & Word Recognition

### MATATAG Alignment
- **Subdomain 3**: Phonics

### Description
Letter-sound relationships and decoding skills

### Learning Competencies (Grade 3)

#### A. Letter-Sound Correspondences
- Know all consonant and vowel sounds
- Identify long and short vowel sounds
- Recognize silent letters (e.g., knife, lamb)

#### B. Phonics Patterns
- **Consonant Blends**: bl, cr, dr, fl, gr, pl, pr, sl, st, tr
- **Consonant Digraphs**: ch, sh, th, wh, ph, ck
- **Vowel Digraphs**: ai, ay, ea, ee, oa, ow
- **Diphthongs**: oi, oy, ou, ow
- **R-controlled vowels**: ar, er, ir, or, ur

#### C. Decoding Skills
- Decode CVC words (cat, dog, sit)
- Decode CVCC words (jump, tent, milk)
- Decode CCVC words (stop, grab, flip)
- Decode multi-syllabic words (2-3 syllables)
- Apply syllabication rules

#### D. Word Recognition
- Read high-frequency sight words (Dolch Grade 3 list)
- Recognize common word patterns
- Read compound words (sunshine, rainbow)
- Decode words with affixes (re-, un-, -ing, -ed, -er, -est)

### Activity Types
- üìù Phonics drills and word building
- üéØ Sight word practice
- üî§ Syllable division exercises
- üìö Decodable text reading
- üéÆ Word pattern matching games

### Mastery Criteria
- 90% accuracy in decoding grade-level words
- Automatically recognize 200+ sight words
- Successfully apply phonics rules

## üìö MODULE 3: Vocabulary & Word Study

### MATATAG Alignment
- **Subdomain 4**: Word Study
- **Subdomain 6**: Vocabulary

### Description
Building word knowledge and understanding meanings

### Learning Competencies (Grade 3)

#### A. Vocabulary Development
- **High-Frequency Words**: 300+ sight words
- **Content-Specific Vocabulary**: Science, math, social studies terms
- **Academic Vocabulary**: Compare, describe, explain, summarize

#### B. Word Meanings
- Use context clues to determine word meanings
- Identify synonyms (happy/joyful, big/large)
- Identify antonyms (hot/cold, tall/short)
- Understand homonyms/homophones (to/two/too, there/their)
- Recognize multiple-meaning words (bat, rock, play)

#### C. Word Structure
- **Compound Words**: playground, butterfly, bedroom
- **Contractions**: can't, won't, I'm, they're
- **Prefixes**: re-, un-, pre-, dis-
- **Suffixes**: -er, -est, -ing, -ed, -ly, -ful
- **Root Words**: Basic Greek and Latin roots

#### D. Word Families and Patterns
- -at family (cat, hat, mat, rat)
- -an family (can, man, pan, ran)
- -it family (sit, hit, bit, lit)
- Other common patterns

### Activity Types
- üìñ Vocabulary in context exercises
- üîç Word meaning matching
- üß© Prefix/suffix building
- üìù Synonym and antonym games
- üí° Context clue practice

### Mastery Criteria
- Use 500+ words receptively
- Apply context clues effectively (75% accuracy)
- Correctly use common affixes

## ‚úèÔ∏è MODULE 4: Grammar & Sentence Construction

### MATATAG Alignment
- **Subdomain 5**: Grammar Awareness

### Description
Understanding sentence structure and language rules

### Learning Competencies (Grade 3)

#### A. Parts of Speech
- **Nouns**: Common, proper, singular, plural
- **Verbs**: Action verbs, helping verbs, linking verbs
- **Adjectives**: Descriptive words, colors, sizes
- **Pronouns**: I, you, he, she, it, we, they, me, him, her
- **Prepositions**: in, on, at, under, over, beside

#### B. Sentence Structure
- **Simple Sentences**: Subject + verb (The dog barks.)
- **Compound Sentences**: Use "and," "but," "or" (I like apples, but I love oranges.)
- **Sentence Types**:
  - Declarative (statements)
  - Interrogative (questions)
  - Exclamatory (exclamations)
  - Imperative (commands)

#### C. Grammar Rules
- **Subject-Verb Agreement**: The cat runs. The cats run.
- **Verb Tenses**:
  - Present tense (I play)
  - Past tense (I played)
  - Future tense (I will play)
- **Singular vs. Plural**: Book/books, child/children
- **Possessive Forms**: 's (Anna's book)

#### D. Mechanics
- **Capitalization**:
  - Beginning of sentences
  - Proper nouns (names, places)
  - Days, months
  - Titles

- **Punctuation**:
  - Period (.)
  - Question mark (?)
  - Exclamation point (!)
  - Comma (in lists, dates, addresses)
  - Apostrophe (contractions, possessives)

### Activity Types
- ‚úçÔ∏è Sentence building exercises
- üîß Grammar error correction
- üéØ Parts of speech identification
- üìã Punctuation practice
- üß© Sentence combining activities

### Mastery Criteria
- Construct grammatically correct simple sentences
- Identify basic parts of speech (80% accuracy)
- Use correct punctuation in writing
- Apply subject-verb agreement

## üìò MODULE 5: Reading Comprehension

### MATATAG Alignment
- **Subdomain 7**: Comprehending and Analyzing Texts

### Description
Understanding, analyzing, and responding to texts

### Learning Competencies (Grade 3)

#### A. Reading Fluency
- Read grade 3 texts with accuracy (95%+)
- Read with appropriate speed (80-100 wpm)
- Read with expression and appropriate phrasing
- Self-monitor and self-correct errors

#### B. Comprehension Strategies
- **Before Reading**: Preview, predict, activate prior knowledge
- **During Reading**: Visualize, question, make connections
- **After Reading**: Summarize, retell, reflect

#### C. Literal Comprehension
- Identify main idea and supporting details
- Recall sequence of events
- Identify characters, setting, and plot
- Answer who, what, when, where, why questions
- Recall facts and details

#### D. Inferential Comprehension
- Make predictions
- Draw conclusions
- Identify cause and effect
- Make inferences about characters' feelings/motivations
- Compare and contrast information

#### E. Critical Thinking
- Distinguish fact from opinion
- Identify author's purpose (inform, entertain, persuade)
- Make text-to-self connections
- Make text-to-text connections

#### F. Text Types (MATATAG Requirement: 70% Narrative, 30% Informational)
- **Narrative Texts (70%)**:
  - Short stories
  - Fables and folktales
  - Personal narratives
  - Fiction passages

- **Informational Texts (30%)**:
  - Simple articles
  - Biographies
  - How-to texts
  - Science/social studies passages

### Activity Types
- üìñ Reading passages with questions
- üé≠ Story element identification
- üîç Main idea and details exercises
- üìä Graphic organizers (story maps, Venn diagrams)
- üí≠ Prediction and inference activities
- üìù Summarizing practice

### Mastery Criteria
- 80% comprehension accuracy on grade-level texts
- Can identify main idea and 3 supporting details
- Successfully makes inferences
- Reads fluently at 80+ wpm

### MATATAG Alignment
- **Subdomain 8**: Creating and Composing Texts

### Description
Writing and creative composition skills

### Learning Competencies (Grade 3)

#### A. Sentence Writing
- Write complete simple sentences
- Write compound sentences using conjunctions
- Use correct capitalization and punctuation
- Apply subject-verb agreement

#### B. Paragraph Writing
- Write a topic sentence
- Add 2-3 supporting details
- Write a closing sentence
- Maintain focus on one topic

#### C. Narrative Writing
- Write a story with beginning, middle, and end
- Include characters and setting
- Use sequence words (first, next, then, finally)
- Use dialogue with proper punctuation
- Write personal narratives

#### D. Informational Writing
- Write simple "how-to" instructions
- Describe objects, people, or places
- Write simple reports with facts
- Use text features (headings, lists)

#### E. Creative Writing
- Write simple poems (rhyming couplets, acrostics)
- Write friendly letters
- Create captions for pictures
- Write journal entries

#### F. Writing Process
- Pre-writing: Brainstorming, planning
- Drafting: Getting ideas on paper
- Revising: Making content better
- Editing: Fixing errors
- Publishing: Final copy

### Activity Types

- üìù Paragraph writing exercises
- üìñ Story writing prompts

## üìä Implementation Recommendations for LiteRise App

### Priority Order (Based on Placement Test Results)
The app currently uses adaptive ordering based on student performance. Continue this approach:

1. **Lowest-performing domain** ‚Üí Highest priority
2. **Second-lowest** ‚Üí Second priority
3. Continue ordering by weakness
4. **Strongest domain** ‚Üí Lowest priority (optional/enrichment)

### Suggested Module Progression
For students without placement test data, use this default sequence:

**Foundational Skills (Must Master First)**
1. Module 2: Phonics & Word Recognition
2. Module 3: Vocabulary & Word Study

**Language Structure**
3. Module 1: Oral Language & Phonological Awareness
4. Module 4: Grammar & Sentence Construction

**Application Skills**
5. Module 5: Reading Comprehension
6. Module 6: Writing & Composition (Advanced)

### Lesson Structure Per Module
Each module should contain:
- **10-15 lessons** progressing from easy to difficult
- **Practice exercises** after each lesson
- **Mini-assessments** every 3-5 lessons
- **Module final assessment**
- **Badge/reward** upon completion

### Content Distribution
- **70% Narrative texts** (stories, fables, fiction)
- **30% Informational texts** (articles, how-to, expository)

This aligns with MATATAG Key Stage 1 requirements.
